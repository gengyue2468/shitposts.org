You have requested a resource that does not exist.[^ We are not sure whether “requested” is the right word. You may have clicked a link. You may have typed a URL. You may have been sent here by a search engine. We do not know. We are only the 404 page.]

We will now elaborate.

---

## On the nature of the error

The HTTP status code 404 means “Not Found.” It means that the server—or in our case, the static file that was generated at build time—has nothing to offer at the path you specified.[^ We are a static site. We do not have a server in the dynamic sense. We have files. One of those files is this one.] It does not mean that the thing you were looking for never existed. It does not mean that it will never exist. It means only that at this moment, at this path, there is nothing.[note: There is, in fact, this page. So there is something. But the something is a page that says there is nothing. The recursion is intentional. We think.]

We have chosen to present this fact in a long form. We have chosen to add many sidenotes. We have chosen not to optimize for the reader who wants to leave quickly.[^ That reader may scroll to the end. There they will find links. We could have put the links at the top. We did not. We are consistent with the rest of the site in this regard.]

---

## On the possible causes

There are several reasons why you might be reading this page.[^ “Several” is a vague word. We use it on purpose. We are not going to enumerate every reason. We are going to suggest a few and then move on.]

First, the page may have been moved. We may have changed a URL. We may have removed a route. We do not maintain a changelog of such decisions.[note: If we did, the changelog would be long and would itself be a candidate for the same low-information-density treatment. We have not written that changelog.] Second, you may have mistyped the address. Typos are common. We do not have a “did you mean” suggestion system. We have this page.[^ We could add such a system. We have not. The addition would require logic. We are a static site. We prefer to keep things static.]

Third, the link that brought you here may have been wrong. It may have been written by a human who made a mistake. It may have been written by a model that hallucinated a path.[^ We are not accusing anyone. We are merely noting that links can be wrong. We have seen it before.] Fourth, you may have arrived here on purpose, to see what the 404 page looks like. If so, welcome. You have found it. It looks like this.

## On what you might do next

We are required by convention to suggest alternatives.[^ “Required” is strong. No one has required us. We have required ourselves. We are following a pattern. We find patterns comforting.] You may wish to return to the [Editorial](/) page, which is the home page of this site and which contains a long introduction that says very little.[note: We have said this about the Editorial page before. We are saying it again. Repetition is part of the style.] You may wish to visit the [Scope](/scope) page, which explains, at great length and with many sidenotes, what this publication is and what it is not.[^ The Scope page is also long. It also has low information density. We are consistent.] You may wish to visit the [Researches](/researches) page, which lists the researches we have published, such as they are.[^ “Such as they are” is doing a lot of work. The researches are what they are. We do not claim more.]

You may also close this tab. You may navigate away. You may forget that you were ever here. All of these are valid.[^ We are not tracking your choice. We are a static site. We do not have analytics by default. If we did, we would say so.]

## On the absence of a contact form

If you believe that something should exist at this path—that a page was removed by error, or that a link is broken and should be fixed—you may be correct.[^ We are not conceding that you are correct. We are conceding that you may be.] We do not provide a contact form on this page.[note: We do not provide a contact form on most pages. We are a static site. Contact forms require server-side logic. We have chosen not to have that.] Instead, there is a simple, old-fashioned address: **contact@shitposts.org**. Messages sent there may accumulate like geological layers of intention: some will be read, some will be skimmed, some will be silently archived.[^ An inbox is a kind of stratigraphy. We are not committed to excavating every layer.]

If you choose to write, we may read your message. We may not respond. We may respond in a way that does not address your concern. We have no policy.[^ We have policies about very few things. One of them is: make the 404 page long. We have followed that policy.]

## On concluding the 404

We have now explained, in more words than were strictly necessary, that the page you requested does not exist; that there are several possible reasons; and that you may go to the Editorial, Scope, or Researches pages, or you may leave.[^ We have also repeated ourselves. We have also added many sidenotes. We have done what we set out to do.]

If you would like to leave, you may use the links above, or you may use the navigation at the top of the site, or you may close the window.[note: We have now said “you may” many times. We are giving you permission. We are also giving you options. We hope that at least one of them is satisfactory.] If you would like to stay, you may stay. This page will not change. It is static. It was generated at build time. It will remain as it is until the next build.[^ We do not know when the next build will be. We are not the build system. We are the 404 page.]

Thank you for reading, or for scrolling, or for having skipped to the end.[^ If you skipped to the end, you may have missed the links. They are in the section “On what you might do next.” You may scroll up. You may not. We leave that to you.]
