## Preliminary Confusions and Orienting Definitions

Before proceeding to substantive analysis, terminological housekeeping requires attention.

By _annunciator_ we designate any electromechanical speech-generation system—whether playback-driven (recorded human voice) or synthesis-driven (concatenative or parametric text-to-speech)—deployed in service of sequential numeric audience summoning within bounded institutional space. [^ Sidenote: Bounded crucial; outdoor or semi-outdoor queue-calling systems exhibit divergent acoustic profiles warranting separate treatment.]

By _call-forward event_ we designate individual utterance instances comprising the canonical syntactic template prevalent in North American anglophone administrative contexts:

```
[NOW SERVING] [NUMBER] [NUMERAL] [AT] [WINDOW] [NUMERAL]
```

Variants observed include insertion discourse markers (_"please proceed to..."_), number-system differences (_"ticket A-47"_), and window identification variation (_"counter"_, _"station"_, _"booth"_). [note: Booth now archaic in most jurisdictions; counter persists in banking contexts.] These variants constitute what we term the _annunciative dialect continuum_—geographically inflected but mutually intelligible production differences within a global standard utterance family.

Confusion initially confronted concerned whether to treat call-forward events as signals or symbols. Pure signal-processing approaches prioritize acoustic properties—fundamental frequency contour, formant structure, signal-to-noise ratio at reception point—treating semantic content as secondary or irrelevant to intelligibility assessment. Purely semiotic approaches invert this hierarchy, treating "number forty-seven" as significatory token whose transmission clarity matters only derivatively.

[^ Sidenote: Neither approach proves adequate; hence our synthesizing framework treats call-forward events as signal-symbol hybrids requiring integrated analysis.]

Prolonged wavering on this matter yielded an orienting principle we provisionally advance as:

> **Hybridity Postulate:** Bureaucratic annunciators simultaneously function as carriers (signals transmitting referents) AND performances (symbols instantiating institutional authority). Analysis must accommodate both functions without reduction.

This postulate—while perhaps seeming banal upon initial presentation [note: Banality in foundational principles no disqualification; solid ground often appears obvious only retrospectively.] —enables subsequent analytical moves that would otherwise violate disciplinary norms in either constituent field.

## The Annunciative Fidelity Index (AFI): Construction and Rationale

Quantifying relationship between acoustic properties and functional adequacy requires indexical construct amenable both to direct measurement within individual sites and comparative aggregation across sites.

The Annunciative Fidelity Index proceeds from classical signal-to-noise ratio formulations while incorporating corrections for:

* **Room harmonic multiplier:** Large enclosed spaces with parallel surfaces develop standing wave patterns selectively amplifying/attenuating frequencies near room-mode values; speaker placement relative to node/antinode positions determines which phonetically-critical frequency bands undergo enhancement versus suppression.

* **Grille occlusion coefficient:** Accumulated dust/debris within ceiling-speaker perforation matrices progressively reduces high-frequency transmission efficiency—affecting consonant-cluster perception disproportionately relative to vowel nuclei.

* **Competitive spectral density:** Presence and amplitude distribution of concurrent environmental sounds—institutional fluorescent lighting ballast hum; HVAC duct resonance; incidental conversation babble; competing electronic tone chimes—contribute masking spectra whose overlap with announcer fundamental frequencies modulates effective intelligibility.

[note: Modulates—linear model provisional; non-linear masking interactions likely dominant under high competitive density conditions.]

Mathematical formulation proceeds as:

**AFI = log₁₀(SNR × RHM × GOC^{-1}) × CSD^γ**

Where γ represents empirically-calibrated competitive-sensitivity exponent currently estimated at approximately 0.73 based on fitting against collected perceptual-intelligibility ratings obtained from experimental subjects exposed to manipulated announcement recordings.

[^ Sidenote: Subjects were volunteers recruited from university populations reporting prior extensive waiting-room exposure—a selection criterion conferring relevant perceptual calibration.]

Derivation details occupy Appendix-like Digressions below; for immediate purposes note that AFI ranges typically span approximately 0.8 to 2.4 across sampled facilities—with lower values indicating degraded fidelity warranting institutional remediation attention per maintenance protocols documented in equipment leasing agreements commonly structured around minimum AFI thresholds specified by lessor organizations.

## Ritual Dimensions: The Liminal Hesitation Coefficient

Purely acoustic parameters prove insufficient for predicting behavioral outcomes following call-forward events. Extended observation reveals systematic latency between annunciator utterance conclusion and recipient initiation of locomotion toward designated window/counter/station—a delay we term _liminal hesitation interval_ and aggregate into normalized measure designated Liminal Hesitation Coefficient:

**LHC = mean(H_{obs}) / H_{ref}**

Where H_{obs} represents measured hesitation intervals for given subject population/facility context and H_{ref} represents reference baseline established under laboratory-controlled conditions simulating maximally salient announcement delivery [^ Sidenote: Maximal salience achieved through headphones-presented recordings in low ambient-noise conditions with explicit expectancy induction protocols.] .

Values exceeding unity indicate hesitation inflation relative to optimal-response baseline; field measurements commonly yield values ranging 1.4–2.8 indicating systematic tendency toward delayed response across naturalistic administrative waiting contexts.

Several factors contribute:

* **Expectation uncertainty:** Multi-hour waits attenuate confidence that called number genuinely references one's own case file; momentary disbelief manifests as hesitation buffer permitting verification against physical ticket medium.

* **Spatial unfamiliarity:** Visitors unfamiliar with facility layout require additional processing cycles to map announced window identifier onto physical service-counter location within visual field—a mapping operation delayed relative to recognition that number itself constitutes valid summoning target.

* **Ritual pacing:** Informal behavioral conventions operative within specific facilities normalize particular hesitation durations through observational learning mechanisms; prolonged co-waiter exposure shapes expectations about appropriate responsivity timing [note: Appropriate according to unspoken norms never formally articulated but nonetheless enforced through subtle disapproving glances directed at overly-eager or excessively-dilatory responders.] .

Critically for present analysis: LHC correlates positively with prior cumulative wait duration independent of acoustic-fidelity considerations—even among patrons receiving highly-intelligible announcements following extended waits, hesitation intervals systematically exceed those exhibited by counterparts experiencing equivalent announcements following brief waits.

[^ Sidenote: Finding robust across controlling for patron age-group categories.]

This finding permits statement of an anticlimactic theorem formalizing folk observation available to any seasoned administrative-service consumer:

> **Wait-Induced Hesitation Inflation Theorem:** For any annunciator system exhibiting adequate fidelity characteristics sufficient for reliable semantic content extraction by typical listener under low-mask conditions, actual post-announcement locomotion initiation latency increases monotonically with cumulative pre-announcement wait duration up-to asymptotic threshold determined by fatigue constraints independent of continued wait-duration increase.

Restated colloquially but without loss of formal validity: people who have waited longer take longer to move when finally called—even when they heard fine.

* Portable digital recorder (24-bit/48kHz minimum specification)
